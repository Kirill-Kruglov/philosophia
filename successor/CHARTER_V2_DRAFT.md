## 2. Phase and information matrix

Three data surfaces: **T** (tuning), **Q** (qualification), **C**
(confirmation). An optional fourth sealed nuisance surface **S** exists
only if separately signed (§6); the draft default is **no S**.

| Surface | Worlds / randomness | Mutable | Readable | Rerunnable | Citable for C1–C6 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **T** | T-partition of the signed frame; public successor dev-root, `("P2","T",…)` domains | everything: learner, optimizer, policy, interface, candidate stacks | everything, including informal arm contrasts | unlimited within the signed envelope | never; `dev-non-citable` forever |
| **Q** | Q-partition; **per attempt, one immutable sample from randomness that does not exist until after durable candidate-manifest and attempt-claim freeze — never from the public dev-root** | nothing (frozen manifest) | validity, the competence binary, predeclared resource-eligibility fields; series not persisted | every launch charged (§4); worlds never reused or redrawn | never; gate assurance only |
| **C** | C-partition; escrow-secret root generated **only after the complete preregistration lock** | nothing | nothing before authorized unsealing | one-shot; validity-first | the only citable surface |

**Q freshness ordering (normative, in temporal order):**

1. the Q frame, sampler, domain map, strata, and no-redraw contract
   exist first (§3, §4);
2. the candidate manifest and the attempt claim become durable
   (committed, git-tracked, hash-recorded in an atomic
   claim-before-draw);
3. only then does the attempt's unpredictable randomness come into
   existence;
4. one immutable Q sample is generated; candidate code receives only
   the sealed contact interface — never the seed, generator state,
   world identities, or any forbidden truth;
5. the attempt is charged even if generation or execution becomes
   invalid (§4);
6. worlds are never reused or redrawn; the seed/world record may become
   public only after the attempt's terminal artifact is durable.

The concrete unpredictability mechanism — a precommitted **post-freeze
public beacon** (valid only if the beacon event and deterministic
mapping are committed before the beacon is realized and no one can
choose among beacon events) versus a **one-shot sealed root** under
reviewed procedural custody — is a later signed implementation cell of
the Q contract. Whichever is chosen must satisfy the conditional
independence property:

```text
W_Q,j ⫫ (candidate_j, all T/Q history before attempt j)
  given the precommitted Q frame, sampler, strata, and attempt id.
```

## 3. Pre-T population and construct contract

Before any real T world generation, a signed contract fixes — with
ownership and freeze timing, values open where noted:

1. the elementary world / inferential block, including target/donor
   roles and the treatment potential outcomes attached to it;
2. the versioned construct generator or finite sampling frame, with
   admissible parameter support and exclusion rules;
3. the T/Q/C partition rule and domain separation — **set disjointness
   is never called exchangeability**;
4. the target C measure `P_C`, or finite-frame strata, inclusion
   probabilities, analysis weights, and finite-population correction;
5. the Q target measure `P_Q`, with either `P_Q = P_C` at the
   superpopulation and stratum-weight level or a predeclared
   transport/conservatism relation that makes Q competence informative
   about C spendability;
6. the candidate/world interaction estimand and any predeclared
   heterogeneity or stratum summaries, with their multiplicity
   ownership (§7);
7. whether learner seeds are fixed and conditioned on or sampled from
   a locked seed law;
8. **exactly one C interpretation** — fixed-frame census, probability
   sample from a fixed finite frame, or named superpopulation sample —
   an author cell whose *type* is chosen before T, not after results.

Realized T, Q, (S,) and C units are disjoint. A generator, support,
strata, or weight change after this freeze **defines a new estimand**
and requires a loud pre-data amendment; it is never another draw from
the old population. The C sampler, strata, weights, and root-generation
protocol exist **before T**; the secret root value and realized C
membership do not exist until after the complete scientific lock, and
are never redrawn (§9).

**Why T remains genuinely open:** the contract freezes the *world side*
(unit, generator version, support, target measures) and nothing on the
*learner side*. T may explore any learner, optimizer, acquisition
policy, interface encoding, and candidate stack on T units without
constraint. What T cannot do is silently move the world to fit the
learner: any world-construct change is a loud amendment, and T
world-usage supports no population claim regardless.

## 4. Qualification: family guarantee, freshness, candidate equivalence

**Family false-pass guarantee.** Before any candidate manifest is
registered for qualification, the signed Q contract fixes: a finite
total launch cap; a finite per-canonical-candidate cap; a family
competence false-pass tolerance `δ_Q`; and an attempt-wise
alpha-spending or always-valid sequential rule with, conditional on all
adaptive prior history,

```text
Pr(Q_PASS_j | H_{<j}, candidate_j is Q-incompetent) ≤ α_j,
Σ_j α_j ≤ δ_Q over every launchable attempt,
⇒ Pr(any Q-incompetent candidate ever passes) ≤ δ_Q.
```

This is a gate assurance that spending C is reasonable — never a C1
claim, never citable.

**Unified charging (strict rule; no uncharged recovery pool).** Every
launched attempt consumes its immutable attempt id, its total-cap
slot, and its error allocation — **including environment-, resource-,
process-, hash-, or seal-invalid launches.** Recovery means only a
later, new, separately claimed attempt permitted by the remaining
caps; never deletion, replacement, reuse, redraw, or automatic rerun.
Invalidity cannot erase a look, and crash-and-retry consumes the same
finite budget as everything else.

**Canonical candidate equivalence.** Candidate identity is the
canonicalized executable scientific manifest (learner code commit,
optimizer, policy, interface, config, device/software stack).
Behavior-inert edits (names, comments, packaging, timestamps,
serialization order) never reset a cap; any change that can alter Q or
C potential outcomes creates a **new candidate** that still draws from
the common total cap.

**Invariant competence predicate.** Q is a population assurance gate:
under the exact frozen candidate and stack, the
probability/mean of achieving the operational certificate within the
fixed Q horizon on a fresh unit from `P_Q` exceeds one common minimum
needed to make C spendable. Before any candidate is registered, the Q
contract fixes the null, unit, aggregation, certificate, horizon,
threshold, sample size or confidence guarantee, `δ_Q`, validity rules,
and missing/non-finite handling. One rule for every candidate,
equivalence class, attempt, and stack. **Competence numerics are
candidate-blind:** they may not be chosen or justified from any
candidate's observed T behavior, informal T arm contrast, or
candidate-specific outcome, and may not move after any Q attempt
(moving them voids the Q contract without preserving prior
qualifiers). Stack-specific wall/memory/reproducibility limits are
**separate engineering-validity gates** and can never weaken the
scientific competence floor. The complete Q contract is signed before
the first candidate is registered and is immutable after the first
launch.

## 5. Candidate identity, qualification timing, and promotion

- The full manifest — including the device/software stack — freezes and
  becomes durable **before that attempt's Q randomness exists** (§2
  ordering). There is no later stack selection anywhere.
- **Serial queue, automatic promotion (charter rule; draft default
  mandated by both reviews):** Q attempts are strictly serial in a
  durable ledger; candidate submission order is durable; **the first
  valid `Q_PASS` automatically closes Q and promotes the exact tested
  candidate and stack.** No simultaneous attempts; no author selection
  after Q; promotion carries the qualified manifest unchanged — it
  records, it does not choose again.
- **No Q statistic beyond validity, the competence binary, and
  predeclared resource-eligibility fields may enter promotion.**
- Any behavior-relevant change after the qualifying attempt is a new
  candidate requiring a new charged attempt; a changed stack is a new
  candidate; a post-lock stack change **voids the lock**.
- Adaptive T still decides which candidate is submitted next; that is
  exactly the selection the §1c conditionality prices.
- An optional non-citable off-CPU Level-0-style breathing check inside
  T remains an **author cell** (qualification owns competence
  assurance; the breathing check would be engineering comfort only and
  never transfers Level 0's CPU result).

## 6. Planning contract: no-scout default, strict-S alternative

**Draft default: no scout.** The programme has three data surfaces.
Confirmatory planning numerics obtain their provenance as follows:

- **sample size:** a precommitted conservative rule — bounded-outcome
  (Popoviciu-type) variance bounds on the locked endpoint scale plus
  the signed resource envelope — owned by the spec gate; conservative
  means possibly larger C, never data-tuned smaller;
- **engineering caps (wall, memory):** label-free resource aggregates
  from Q attempts (Q has no arms; its engineering telemetry carries no
  contrast information) plus platform limits;
- **endpoint horizon, budgets, scientific margins, treatment,
  population, analysis:** never planning-data-informed by any surface;
  owned by their spec gates with non-outcome provenance.

**Signed alternative (strict S):** Kirill may instead sign, before
promotion, a sealed nuisance surface under the strict contract: a
separately domain-separated sample disjoint from T, every Q attempt,
and C, never reused; candidate, stack, C population contract, endpoint
horizon, treatment definitions, contrast family, margins, and analysis
method all frozen **before** S runs; a sealed evaluator computing only
a preregistered nuisance function **proved invariant under every
arm-label permutation and sign reversal of every locked contrast**;
released surface limited to validity, the deterministic sample-size
output (or a prebounded invariant nuisance scalar), and label-free
aggregate engineering resources; no arm-specific censoring, events,
moments, quantiles, losses, scores, series, contrasts, intervals, or
resource fields; raw S data sealed until after C's terminal decision
and permanently non-citable; every S-dependent numeric retains the
conservative fallback above. **S may inform only the blinded
sample-size recalculation and label-free engineering caps — never a
margin or endpoint budget.**

**Honest surface count:** under the default, the architecture is
three data surfaces (T/Q/C) and
`I_ACCEPT_THREE_SURFACE_PHASE_ARCHITECTURE` is accurate. Signing S adds
a fourth sealed nuisance surface and **amends that token** (re-signed
as three epistemic roles plus one sealed nuisance instrument). The
default is chosen for lower governance cost and a strictly smaller
information surface — an architecture-cost judgment, **not** an
evidence-driven choice; no data selected it.

## 7. Multiplicity ownership (pre-data, charter-fixed)

One promoted candidate and one C execution remove candidate-level
reconfirmation; they do not remove within-confirmation multiplicity.
Before any data relevant to a family, the successor claim graph and
level specs assign:

1. **C1/contact family:** every ACTIVE/YOKED/static superiority,
   equivalence, and non-superiority predicate used for C1 or
   operational contact-mode selection — jointly controlled
   (simultaneous intervals, closed testing, or a locked hierarchical
   rule). Static can never become an undeclared alternative route to a
   positive C1.
2. **C2–C4 cascade family:** every superiority/equivalence/
   non-superiority, presentation, anchor, ledger, and placebo predicate
   in the priority cascade — joint coverage or closed gatekeeping.
3. **C5 family:** every registered path-credit contrast and any
   claim-capable subgroup/robustness predicate.
4. **C6:** annotation-only, never decisive; any inferential C6
   subclaim gets its own controlled family and can never grant, veto,
   or rescue proof.
5. **Proof composition:** `PROOF_CORE` and `PROOF_STRONG` consume only
   validly controlled component decisions under a preregistered
   conjunction/gatekeeping rule; no unregistered contrast, endpoint,
   subgroup, or repeated analysis may earn a claim; reusing C data for
   unregistered routes is forbidden.

Numeric alphas and margins wait for their owning pre-data spec gates;
family membership, primary/secondary status, and control-method
ownership do not wait for results.

## 8. Exclusive terminal state machine

| Surface | Valid terminal / ending | Invalid terminal | Meaning and recovery |
|---|---|---|---|
| T | `T_ENVELOPE_EXHAUSTED`, `T_AUTHOR_STOP`, or candidate submitted | typed environment/resource/process/hash failure in T infrastructure | engineering only; T recovers inside its signed envelope; no C1–C6 field exists |
| Q attempt | `Q_VALID_PASS` or `Q_VALID_FAIL` after complete valid evaluation | `Q_INVALID:<ENVIRONMENT\|RESOURCE\|PROCESS\|HASH\|SEAL>`, competence binary **unset** | one common competence rule; **every launch consumes its id, error allocation, and cap slot, including invalid launches**; recovery only as a later new charged attempt; no deletion/replacement/reuse/redraw/automatic rerun |
| Q phase | first valid pass → automatic promotion; or `Q_CAP_EXHAUSTED_NO_QUALIFIER` | contract/hash failure may end the phase | no-qualifier is a **process ending** — never scientific `INSUFFICIENT`, a C1 boundary, or evidence that no competent learner exists |
| C escrow | one valid attested ciphertext/root commitment | `C_ESCROW_INVALID:<cause>` | malformed escrow ends that artifact; no scientific field; no automatic regeneration |
| C execution | only a valid completed run may set scientific pass, null, boundary, `INSUFFICIENT`, or the spec's censoring terminal | `C_INVALID:<ENVIRONMENT\|RESOURCE\|PROCESS\|HASH\|SEAL>`, all scientific fields **unset** | no invalid state is censoring or a boundary; no automatic rerun |

**Missing and non-finite states are classified by ownership before
data:** a correctly executed learner divergence may count as
`Q_VALID_FAIL` (or C scientific censoring) only where the signed
predicate/endpoint explicitly says so; hardware or numerical-
environment failure is invalidity; a missing required observation or
malformed report is process invalidity unless a missingness rule was
explicitly part of the endpoint. Scientific censoring exists only
after every validity, seal, completeness, and persistence predicate
passes. Numeric thresholds for these classifications are later cells;
their ownership is not. No process state may ever be narrated as a
wall, a scientific boundary, or learner impossibility.
