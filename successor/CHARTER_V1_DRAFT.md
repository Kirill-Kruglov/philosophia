# Successor line charter — v1 draft

Status: `CHARTER_DRAFT_FOR_XY_REVIEW`. Drafted under the Route B author
decision (`canonical/AUTHOR_ROUTE_DECISION.md`, commit `e4d0c0c`, five
tokens). This charter defines governance for a successor scientific
line. It authorizes nothing executable: no learner choice, device
choice, world registry, implementation, entropy draw, development run,
scout, escrow, lock, confirmatory run, outcome, or claim transition.
Every numeric cell is explicitly open (§11) and unsigned.

## 1. Identity and scope

- **Working identifier:** `philosophia2` (the final name is Kirill's
  voice decision, not a charter cell). **Recommended namespace:** a new
  repository, `philosophia2`, whose `INHERITANCE.md` pins the stopped
  line by commit hash. Fallback if a single repository is preferred: a
  top-level `successor/` tree with its own manifest — but the separate
  repository is the stronger guarantee that the successor cannot be
  narrated as a continuation.
- **Scientific scope:** the same programme question — can a derivable
  algebraic world provide primary experience to a small learner — with
  chosen contact (C1) first-class and `PROOF_STRONG` reachable in
  principle. The successor tests a **successor estimand version**: its
  learner class, world construction, budgets, and margins will be its
  own, defined at its own spec time, never inherited from the censored
  records.
- **What the successor is not:** it is not Level 1 v3, not a retry, not
  a reinterpretation of the v1/v2 feasibility records, and not evidence
  about the stopped line. The stopped line remains the stopped-open
  publication record with programme claim `OPEN`.
- **Fixed construct (charter-level):** contact with hidden finite
  algebraic structures through a narrow truth oracle, with a
  chosen-contact versus yoked-contact comparison as the C1 skeleton and
  a fixed-budget right-censored budget-to-certified-competence endpoint
  class. Changing this construct (e.g., abandoning algebraic worlds or
  the oracle-contact form) exceeds this charter and requires a new one.

## 3. Claim semantics

Inherited by **fresh signature** (no silent carry-over), verbatim in
meaning:

- `PROOF_CORE = C2 ∧ C3 ∧ C4`; `PROOF_STRONG = PROOF_CORE ∧ C1 ∧ C5`;
- C6 is a mandatory reported annotation, never a verdict conjunct;
- unresolved required comparisons route to `INSUFFICIENT`, never to
  success or boundary;
- a C1 negative routes to a **named scoped boundary** re-derived for
  the successor's own contact construction at spec time (the old
  `…AT_DISTANCE_1` scope does not automatically transfer);
- censored/`UNKNOWN` never success; certificate or competence failure
  never proves the learner lacked the structure;
- Arm-D-class inherited-ledger semantics carry forward when Level-2
  analogs are chartered.

**New rule (successor-specific, load-bearing):** *no tuning or
qualification result can earn, kill, or boundary-label any of C1–C6.*
Only the confirmation surface can move a scientific claim, and only
under its lock.

**Named honesty clause:** because tuning is an open sandbox (§5), the
promoted design will have been selected partly for showing promise in
development. The successor's confirmatory claim is therefore explicitly
**conditional on that selection**: it tests whether the selected
design's effect survives on held-out worlds under a preregistered
analysis. This is the standard confirmatory structure; the stopped line
avoided selection and paid with a one-shot competence gate. The
successor names selection and pays with disjoint confirmation.

## 4. Phase and data boundary matrix

Three surfaces. Two are insufficient: without qualification, competence
is either discovered inside the confirmatory contract (the stopped
line's architecture, rejected by the Route B decision) or asserted from
tuning worlds the candidate may have overfit. Secrecy alone does not
prevent selection leakage; disjointness plus fixed promotion rules do.

| Surface | Worlds | Mutable | Readable | Rerunnable | Citable |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **T — tuning development** | T-partition of the construct's parameter space; public successor dev-root, `("P2","T",…)` domains | everything: learner, optimizer, acquisition policy, interface, candidate device stacks | everything: losses, series, informal arm contrasts | unlimited within the declared T budget | **never** for C1–C6; engineering notes only, permanently labeled `dev-non-citable` |
| **Q — qualification** | Q-partition, disjoint from T; a **fresh, never-reused draw per attempt** from `("P2","Q",attempt,…)` domains under the public dev-root | nothing — the candidate (full config manifest + code commit, hashed) is frozen before its attempt | the competence **binary**, validity fields, and resource aggregates only — series are not persisted (the stopped line's A8 allowed-surface pattern, inherited) | attempts capped (§11); a failed candidate returns to T and re-enters only as a **new candidate id** | **never** for C1–C6; gate facts only |
| **C — confirmation** | C-partition, disjoint from T and Q; **escrow-secret root generated only after the scientific lock** | nothing | nothing before the authorized outcome unsealing | one-shot; validity-first invalidity routes, fail-closed | the **only** citable surface |

The partition of the construct's parameter space into T/Q/C strata is
fixed and signed **before any T generation** (boundaries are open cells,
§11). Q and C parameters never appear in T; C parameters never appear
in T or Q. All three partitions instantiate the same construct, so
qualification is informative about confirmation without touching it.

## 5. Development and qualification contract

- **Admissible in T:** any learner architecture, optimizer, training
  policy, acquisition rule, interface encoding, and candidate device
  stack; world *construct* fixed (§1). Changing the world class is a
  loud charter amendment, never silent drift.
- **T budget and stopping:** a declared T envelope (compute/wall/
  calendar; open cell) with scheduled review checkpoints; T ends by
  envelope exhaustion, author stop (the signed right to say
  "достаточно"), or qualification success. Extension is a loud signed
  decision. Indefinite development is thereby structurally excluded.
- **Candidate registry:** every candidate is a committed manifest
  (architecture, optimizer, policy, device stack, code commit, config
  hash). Q entry requires the frozen registry entry to precede the
  attempt.
- **Qualification entry rule (fixed before any Q result exists):** the
  competence predicate — an operational certificate on Q worlds within
  a fixed budget, endpoint-shaped like the confirmatory endpoint — is
  written and signed before the first attempt. Its numeric cells are
  open (§11) but its *shape* is charter-fixed: certified competence on
  freshly drawn Q worlds, validity-first, binary.
- **Repeat policy:** per-candidate and total attempt caps (open cells);
  every attempt logged in a public attempt ledger; each attempt
  consumes fresh Q worlds. A failed attempt routes the candidate back
  to T.
- **If no candidate qualifies:** the successor line ends as
  "development did not produce a qualifiable learner" — a named,
  publishable process ending. No confirmatory experiment exists, and no
  scientific claim moves. This ending is allowed and honorable.
- **Promotion (exactly one candidate):** multiple candidates may
  qualify; the qualified set closes at promotion; **exactly one**
  candidate is promoted by author signature, committed **before any
  confirmatory artifact exists**. If a pre-registered deterministic
  rule (e.g., first-to-qualify) is preferred over signed selection,
  that choice is itself made before the first attempt. One promoted
  candidate, one confirmatory experiment — multiplicity is structurally
  excluded.
- **Optional design scout (named channel, the only dev→spec bridge):**
  after promotion, before lock, a bounded non-citable scout on
  Q-partition worlds may inform the spec's numeric cells (budget,
  margins, sample size) with **variance, censoring, and resource
  aggregates only — never effect direction**. The spec review audits
  every numeric cell's provenance; any cell traced to an effect-size or
  arm-ordering observation is rejected. This inherits the stopped
  line's scout discipline.
- **Freeze order (strict):** candidate freeze → qualification →
  promotion signature → device-stack freeze (§6; may be co-signed with
  promotion) → successor scientific spec (estimand, margins, analysis,
  stop rules) → X/Y bounded review → author signatures → C-world
  escrow generation → preregistration lock → one-shot confirmatory
  execution. Nothing later in this order may modify anything earlier.

## 6. Device-selection contract

The available host (AMD Ryzen AI Max+ 395, 128 GB unified memory)
offers several stacks. This charter fixes the **procedure**, not the
winner; nothing in the v2 record (35.820 h / 49.782 GiB) selects any
stack or predicts that any learner fits any device.

- **Exploration:** candidate stacks may be freely explored in T.
- **Correctness equivalence:** every candidate stack must reproduce a
  canonical small CPU-float32 oracle fixture (fixed tiny model, fixed
  seeds, few steps) within a declared numerical tolerance (open cell)
  on losses and logits. Exact cross-stack agreement is not required.
- **Within-stack determinism:** the chosen stack must be bitwise
  deterministic against itself (fixed seeds, deterministic kernels) or,
  where a stack cannot offer that, must satisfy a declared bounded
  reproducibility criterion (open cell) — signed as part of the stack
  freeze, never relaxed after it.
- **Checkpoint portability:** checkpoints must round-trip with stable
  state hashes within the frozen stack; cross-device portability is not
  required once the stack is frozen end-to-end.
- **Provenance:** device model, driver, compiler, and library versions
  enter every claim, report, and checkpoint fingerprint.
- **Immutability point:** the stack freezes at (or before) promotion
  signature. A stack change after promotion returns the candidate to
  qualification on the new stack; a stack change after the scientific
  lock **voids the lock** — a new lock (and possibly a new charter, if
  the change alters the construct's feasibility class) is required.
- **Failure routing:** stack faults during confirmation are process
  invalidity under the validity-first taxonomy — never scientific
  evidence, never silent retry.
- **Honest limitation (R-11):** Level 0's earned platform status is
  CPU-specific. A non-CPU successor stack begins with **no
  breathing-platform anchor**; the qualification gate partially
  substitutes, and the spec must not cite Level 0 as capacity evidence
  for any stack.

Charter → X/Y bounded charter review → author charter signature packet
(§12) → lineage bootstrap (repo, manifests, quarantine) → T
infrastructure and T phase → qualification contract concretization
(author signs numeric cells) → Q attempts → promotion + stack freeze

by this charter.

## 8. Invalidity and stop routes

- The validity-first taxonomy is inherited as the universal pattern:
  scientific censoring is a valid observation only on a valid completed
  run; environment/resource-cap/process faults are typed invalidity
  with the scientific field unset; hash/seal violations are whole-check
  fail-closed invalidity; no invalidity subtype authorizes an automatic
  rerun anywhere.
- Malformed escrow (bad generation, failed attestation, broken
  ciphertext) is **process invalidity ending that artifact** — never a
  scientific failure, never silently regenerated; recovery requires an
  explicit signed process decision.
- One-shot surfaces: the C-world escrow generation and the confirmatory
  execution. Everything in T is recoverable by construction; Q attempts
  are capped but individually recoverable as process (a crashed attempt
  is an invalid attempt, not a failed qualification).
- Stop points at which the line may end without a confirmatory
  experiment, each publishable as a process ending: T envelope
  exhaustion; qualification cap exhaustion; author stop; charter
  amendment refusal.

## 9. Artifact and namespace contract

- Successor artifacts live under the successor namespace only; the
  stopped line's repository is never written again by successor work.
- Every scientific artifact: canonical JSON, schema-versioned
  (`philosophia2.*`), hash-chained to its authorization and governing
  documents, with the `lineage` field of §2.
- Dummy/test fixtures derive from declared test-only seeds and are
  fail-closed against emitting real artifacts (escrow attestation
  required), inheriting the stopped line's pattern.
- T/Q worlds derive from a public successor dev-root drawn once by a
  reviewed one-shot driver; C worlds derive from a separate
  escrow-secret root generated only after the lock. Custody follows the
  signed procedural-custody pattern (generator as procedural witness,
  Kirill holding keys; no cryptographic-independence claim).

## 10. Negative authorization surface

This charter does not authorize and its signature will not authorize:
any learner or device choice; any world registry or partition values;
any implementation beyond what later work packages individually
authorize; any entropy draw; any T run; any Q attempt; any promotion;
any scout; any spec; any escrow; any lock; any confirmatory execution;
any claim transition; any citation of T/Q results for C1–C6; any v1/v2
comparison; any reinterpretation of the stopped line. The v1/v2 censors
motivated this charter's phase architecture as a process lesson and
chose nothing in it.

## 11. Open cells (explicitly unsigned)

Final line name; repository location; T/Q/C parameter-partition
boundaries; T budget envelope and checkpoint schedule; qualification
competence predicate numerics (budget, certificate counts, window);
per-candidate and total Q attempt caps; promotion rule variant
(signed selection vs first-to-qualify); device numerical tolerance and
bounded-reproducibility criterion; design-scout allowed-statistics
list; all confirmatory estimand numerics (budget, margins, sample
size, arms' exact construction); escrow environment details. None of
these is chosen here; several must not be chosen until the phase that
owns them.
