## Central design problem

"Development before lock" can repair the one-shot competence bottleneck, but it
can also become an unlimited garden of researcher degrees of freedom. A single
dev/confirmatory split is not automatically sufficient: repeatedly selecting a
learner on the same development registry can overfit the registry, while using a
confirmatory panel to select the learner contaminates the result.

Design the smallest auditable phase architecture that permits honest iteration
and still supports one confirmatory claim. At minimum evaluate a three-surface
structure:

1. **Tuning development:** open iteration; permanently non-citable.
2. **Qualification:** disjoint from tuning, still permanently non-citable;
   demonstrates that the frozen candidate can reach the competence floor before
   a scientific contract exists.
3. **Confirmation:** disjoint from both; generated or exposed only after the
   candidate, device stack, estimand, margins, analysis, and stop rules are
   signed and locked.

You may replace this structure if you can give a smaller one with the same
protection. Do not pretend that secrecy alone prevents selection leakage. Name
exactly what can be inspected, changed, rerun, or promoted in each phase.

## Required risk analysis

At minimum attack:

- development overfitting disguised as competence;
- qualification becoming a citable pilot;
- confirmatory worlds being indirectly selected;
- device choice being outcome-triggered;
- indefinite development until one candidate passes;
- multiple candidates creating an undeclared multiplicity problem;
- inherited Level 1 artifacts leaking the old test;
- changing the learner and world simultaneously so the estimand drifts;
- treating a process gate as a wall or scientific boundary;
- using the successor merely to seek a positive ending.
