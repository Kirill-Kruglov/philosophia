## Direct answers to the six confirmation questions

### 1. Q family guarantee

Yes. Under the v2 contract, for every adaptively submitted attempt `j`,

```text
Pr(Q_PASS_j | H_<j, candidate_j is Q-incompetent) <= alpha_j
sum_j alpha_j <= delta_Q
```

is sufficient to control the probability that any population-incompetent
candidate ever passes, provided the later Q contract instantiates the already
owned meanings exactly: `alpha_j` is predictable from and committed under
`H_<j` before attempt-j randomness; Q incompetence is defined by the common
fixed `P_Q` population predicate; and the attempt test is conditionally valid
for the frozen canonical manifest and stack. A union bound supplies the stated
family guarantee; a predeclared always-valid sequential construction is an
equivalent permitted route.

`H_<j` must include the complete prior T/Q registry, durable submission order,
Q contract and spending state, canonical candidate manifest and stack, attempt
id, prior valid and invalid terminals, and every released eligibility/resource
field. V2's “all adaptive prior history,” manifest rule, and durable ledger own
those variables; the Q contract must serialize them but need not exist now.

There is no reset path:

- every launch spends its attempt id, total-cap slot, and error allocation,
  including invalid launches;
- behavior-inert changes preserve the canonical candidate and its per-class
  cap;
- behavior-relevant changes may create a new candidate but remain within the
  common finite launch cap and the same `delta_Q` spending family;
- crashes, invalidity, and changed stacks cannot delete, replace, reuse, or
  redraw an attempt; and
- the Q sample is generated from post-freeze unpredictable randomness and is
  never reused.

Thus arbitrary adaptive T search changes which frozen candidate is tested; it
does not escape the family guarantee.

### 2. Selected-design estimand

Yes. Section 1c gives the required conditioning and generalization boundary:

- `H_TQ` records the adaptive T/Q selection and promotion history;
- `H_preC` extends it through every permitted planning, specification, review,
  and signature decision in the scientific lock;
- `d* = R(H_TQ)` is the promoted content-addressed candidate;
- `s*` is the exact Q-qualified stack carried unchanged into promotion;
- `L*` fixes the scientific treatments, endpoint, analysis, and seed scope;
- `P_C` is the sole target population or design-weighted finite frame; and
- `selection_scope_id` hashes the conditioning objects into every verdict.

The estimand averages only over worlds and any seed law named by `P_C` and
`L*`. With fixed seeds it is seed-conditioned; with a finite census it is
descriptive of that frame. It licenses no claim about the learner class, search
procedure, Route B generally, or an unselected design.

Automatic promotion of the first valid pass removes the v1 post-Q author-choice
discretion. It does not remove adaptive selection: T still determines the
durable submission order, which is why `H_TQ` remains part of the estimand and
verdict scope.

### 4. No-scout planning

Yes, subject to the conservative interpretation already stated by v2. If an
arm endpoint is bounded as `Y_a in [0,B]`, Popoviciu gives
`Var(Y_a) <= B^2/4`; a paired difference lies in `[-B,B]`, giving the
worst-case bound `Var(Y_YOKED - Y_ACTIVE) <= B^2`. Locked stratum weights and
finite-frame sampling produce corresponding deterministic weighted bounds.
Together with locked margins, family alpha/control method, a predeclared power
or confidence-width target, and the fixed analysis, these bounds can produce a
conservative sample-size rule without outcome data.

The resource envelope is a feasibility constraint, not a statistical discount.
If the bound-derived sample size exceeds the frame or resources, the gate must
report infeasibility or undergo a loud pre-data redesign; it may not reduce N or
weaken a margin in response.

Q has no arms. Its label-free wall, memory, and reproducibility telemetry may
inform engineering caps only. It cannot alter the endpoint or horizon,
scientific margin, population, treatment, contrast family, sample-size analysis,
or inferential rule. Q competence and resource facts remain non-citable for
C1–C6.

### 5. Terminal machine

Yes. Section 8 is exclusive and total at charter level:

- T has separately named envelope exhaustion, author stop, submission, and
  typed engineering invalidity;
- a complete valid Q attempt is exactly `Q_VALID_PASS` or `Q_VALID_FAIL`;
- Q environment/resource/process/hash/seal invalidity leaves the competence
  binary unset while still consuming the launch, alpha allocation, and cap
  slot;
- first valid pass promotes mechanically, while cap exhaustion without a pass
  is `Q_CAP_EXHAUSTED_NO_QUALIFIER`, a process ending rather than scientific
  `INSUFFICIENT`;
- malformed C escrow is typed artifact invalidity with no automatic
  regeneration; and
- only a valid completed C execution may set pass, null, boundary,
  `INSUFFICIENT`, or scientific censoring, while every C process/resource/hash/
  seal invalidity leaves all scientific fields unset.

Missingness and non-finiteness have pre-data owners. Correctly executed learner
divergence may become Q failure or C censoring only if the signed competence
predicate or endpoint says so; environment/numerical failure is invalidity.
There is no process-to-science route, no in-place rerun, and no erased launch.
