## 1. Verdict and decisive reason

**REVISE_PROGRAMME**

Two corrections to my own route map are mandatory before any Level 1 design
or lock, and both are scientific, not editorial:

1. **The Level 1 comparison is not identifiable as framed.** "Equal oracle
   budget AND equal answer entropy" does not define a causal estimand. Equal
   binary answer entropy is not equal information about hidden structure
   (an adversary matches entropy while starving structure); matching full
   realized mutual information is outcome-dependent matching and would
   subtract the very thing choice is supposed to buy. The framed rule
   either under-controls (active wins by choosing an easier query stratum)
   or over-controls (the treatment is matched away). §6 replaces it with a
   yoked-transplant construction that is generatable and auditable before
   outcome.
2. **The route map overstates Level 1's logical role.** `active ≤ static`
   does not kill the manufactured-experience thesis. The programme's clause
   1 requires contact that is *declared and first-hand* rather than
   inherited; it does not require that the learner *chose* it. A static
   first-hand query corpus on the learner's own world still satisfies
   clause 1. Therefore C1-negative is a named boundary coordinate
   (choice does not matter; contact still might), not `FALSIFIED`. The
   original map's "kill: active ≤ static → the core takes a hit at the
   contact node" must be amended by signature to "→ the *choice* claim
   dies; the contact claim routes through Level 2 with static first-hand
   contact."

With these two amendments adopted (signed, loud), Level 1 design is ready.
Everything else in the map survives: the five arms, the fixed curriculum,
the escrow discipline, the register-(b) finish line.

## 5. Formal world and oracle contract (freeze candidate)

Smallest family rich enough for Levels 1–2 without mid-stream ontology
change — everything below enumerable and independently checkable:

- **Hidden object.** Finite abelian groups presented by hidden data:
  development stratum `Z/n` (n from a declared sampler range —
  UNRESOLVED-N1), family rungs fixed by the curriculum generator:
  `Z/n` → `Z/n × Z/m` (coprime pairs) → dihedral `D_n` (first non-abelian
  rung) → broken-axiom variants (sticky generator: L ≠ R⁻¹ on a declared
  defect; quotient traps per line 12) → presentation change (C3). Escrow
  families: same generator, parameter strata *disjoint* from development
  (disjoint residue classes / coprime-pair sets), **generated only after
  lock and escrowed encrypted before any outcome** (line 12 law 8).
- **Query grammar.** Words over generator tokens `{R, L}` (products add
  `{S, T}`) with a declared length cap (UNRESOLVED-N2). One query form:
  `EQ(u, v)` — "do u and v reach the same state from the origin?"
- **Oracle.** Exact truth, cost 1 per query, no other channel. Budgets are
  per-instance and locked.
- **Forbidden truth.** The learner never sees: n or any parameter, the
  relation set, homomorphisms, state names, or any oracle beyond EQ bits.
  Enforced by the Level 0 fail-closed API discipline (learner/eval views).
- **Prediction interface.** EQ prediction on held-out pairs with calibrated
  ABSTAIN. **Reaching truth** (solve criterion, one of two — Kirill
  decision Q6): (a) a designated order-probe protocol answered correctly
  and persistently, or (b) preregistered held-out EQ accuracy with a
  persistence window (Level 0 style). Budget-to-truth = oracle queries
  consumed until the criterion holds persistently.
- **Presentations and the C3 mapping.** Algebra interface: tokens, words,
  EQ. Cayley interface: anonymous vertex ids, single-step edge walks,
  "same-vertex?" oracle on the *same* hidden group with the same generating
  set. Semantics preservation is a theorem (w ↦ vertex reached by walking
  w), and it is *verified*, not assumed: an independent checker enumerates
  both presentations on every sampled instance (sizes are enumerable) and
  certifies the bijection before lock. Line 12's warning is inherited
  verbatim: the A↔G destination agreement is a free sham — C3 is measured
  as behavioral cost reduction, never as agreement between presentations.
- **Generator-leakage tests (preregistered, fail-closed).** (a)
  Shuffled-answer null: with oracle answers shuffled within instance, no
  arm may beat chance on held-out EQ — else the corpus encoding leaks
  structure without contact. (b) Parameter-shift control: shift the
  sampler's parameter distribution without changing semantics; a learner
  whose "laws" merely encode sampler statistics degrades, one with
  structural experience does not (Sol's kill "laws = encoding of hidden
  generator parameters", now operational). (c) Probe-from-encoding: a
  standalone probe trained to read n from the serialized corpus must fail.
- **One-door detection.** Inherited from line 12 as machinery, not
  metaphor: log per-instance failure profiles for all arms/seeds; apply the
  success/failure split — co-adaptation surviving into *correlated
  failures* = shared derivation; success-only convergence = the world's
  one door. A family where every independent arm converges with zero
  co-failure signal is labeled ONE_DOOR and excluded from path-dependence
  claims (C5 especially) rather than misread as path independence or
  dependence.

## 6. Information-matched Level 1 design

**Estimand.** Δ_choice = E over escrowed instances of
[budget-to-truth(ACTIVE) − budget-to-truth(YOKED)], same learner class,
same per-instance oracle budget, same training procedure applied to the
resulting (query, answer) transcript.

**The three arms, per instance:**

1. **ACTIVE** — queries chosen by a *preregistered acquisition rule*
   computed from the learner's own current uncertainty (maximum predicted
   disagreement over the declared grammar). Not a learned policy: keeps
   the treatment auditable and the trajectory deterministic given seed.
2. **YOKED** — the query *sequence* transplanted from an ACTIVE run on a
   different instance under a fixed preregistered derangement σ (instance i
   receives the queries ACTIVE generated on instance σ(i)); answers come
   honestly from instance i's own oracle. Matches query geometry, length
   distribution, stratum balance, and topology; destroys instance-adaptive
   coupling. Generated before outcome by construction; no arm ever sees
   another arm's answers or its own trajectory re-served.
3. **RANDOM-STATIC** — queries drawn from the declared grammar's fixed
   random design. Separates "good geometry" from "any geometry."

**Why this is identifiable where the map's rule was not:** the treatment is
exactly "coupling of query choice to the learner's own answers," because
YOKED holds the query-generation process fixed (it *is* the active
process, displaced one instance) while breaking only the coupling. Label
balance and realized answer entropy are downstream *mediators* of the
treatment and are deliberately not matched — matching them would subtract
the effect under test. Nothing is matched on outcomes; σ and all designs
are fixed at lock; audit = re-derive every YOKED transcript from committed
ACTIVE transcripts and σ.

**Readings.** ACTIVE > YOKED: C1 earned (adaptive coupling matters).
YOKED > RANDOM-STATIC: query geometry matters and is transplantable —
first-hand contact with good geometry, no live choice needed. ACTIVE ≈
YOKED > RANDOM: choice redundant, geometry real → `BOUNDARY_CONTACT_CHOICE`
with a constructive remnant. ACTIVE ≈ YOKED ≈ RANDOM: contact structure
indifferent at this budget — Level 2 proceeds with RANDOM-STATIC contact
(cheapest), and C1 records its boundary.

**What a Level 1 null does and does not falsify.** ACTIVE ≤ YOKED
falsifies: "experiment choice carries experience advantage" in this family
at this budget. It does **not** falsify: manufactured experience (C2–C4),
first-hand-contact value, or the programme. This is now structural (§3),
not interpretive.

**Design mechanics.** Experimental unit = world instance; unit of variance
= instance block (never query, seed, or checkpoint — pseudoreplication
arrives three times, law 3). All three arms run on every instance
(within-instance pairing); seeds crossed with instances by a locked
schedule. Primary endpoint: budget-to-truth (or accuracy-at-budget if Q6
resolves to (b)). Secondary: calibration curve, honest-abstention rate,
confident-lie rate. Nulls: shuffled-answer (must be flat across arms);
leakage probes (§5). Fail-closed: any leakage, budget mismatch, non-finite,
escrow breach. Instance counts and quorum: UNRESOLVED-N3 — set at lock
from a non-outcome variance scout (allowed: measures variance of the
endpoint on development instances only, never compares arms for the
record). Escrow before execution: the holdout instance family, encrypted,
DAG-committed. Claim scope: this family, this budget, this acquisition
rule — never "active learning" in general.

## 7. Decisive Level 2 design and five-arm interpretation

**Structure.** Fixed curriculum (§5 rungs; generator locked, never
learner-selected). Five arms per curriculum sequence, all sharing identical
world sequences, budgets, and escrow families:

| Arm | Weights | Ledger | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | fresh | none | scratch baseline |
| B | transferred | none | weights channel |
| C | transferred | truthful | full experience candidate |
| D | fresh | truthful | ledger-as-text channel |
| E | transferred | false (statistics-matched, semantically corrupted; construction locked) | placebo/traceability control |

**Primary endpoint (C2):** budget-to-truth on escrowed *unseen* families,
same presentation. **C3 endpoint:** budget-to-truth on escrowed instances
under the Cayley presentation, with the renamed-token interface as a
preregistered positive anchor (must transfer; its failure is
`PLATFORM_OR_DESIGN_INVALID`, not a finding). **Contrast algebra (locked
before outcome):**

- C2: C > A on escrow (quorum UNRESOLVED-N4).
- C4 traceability: (C − E) > 0 **and** E ≤ B (false ledger adds nothing
  beyond weights). If E ≈ C → `FALSIFIED_AT_C4` and the C2 reading is
  withdrawn (preregistered poisoning, §4).
- Channel decomposition: B vs A (weights), D vs A (ledger-alone), C vs B
  (ledger beyond weights) → boundary coordinates of §4.
- Forgetting obligation: exact replay reproduction of old worlds measured
  per arm; forgetting faster than acquisition is a named kill from the
  matrix (unchanged).

**Unit and pairing.** Unit = world block within a sequence; arms paired
within block; seeds crossed by locked schedule; variance unit = block.
Counts: UNRESOLVED-N4 (post-L1 variance evidence; no invented power
numbers). **Fail-closed invalidity:** any arm reading another arm's
memory; ledger schema leakage of forbidden truth (the ledger stores
queries, answers, surprise flags — never parameters); escrow breach;
generator leakage. **Escrowed before execution:** unseen families, Cayley
instance set, false-ledger construction seedschedule. **Level 3 reuse:**
metric streams, checkpoints at the Level 0 cadence discipline, per-arm
failure profiles (for one-door detection), full transcripts — all saved
under the outcome harness so C6 needs no new training programme.

**Learner/memory behavioral contract (from §D of the task).** Level 0
scale transformer, from scratch where the arm says fresh; weights persist
only in B, C, E. Ledger schema: append-only (world id, query, answer,
pre-query prediction, surprise flag, provenance hash); the *sparse* ledger
is the surprise-flagged subset (repers = prediction errors, per the core);
full transcript retained for audit only. Replay/consolidation: exact
stored transcripts on a locked schedule; deterministic replay obligations
inherited verbatim from Level 0 (CPU float32, pinned environment,
interlocks, fail-closed views). Confident lie vs honest abstention are
separate logged outcomes with locked thresholds. Resampled paths (for
C5): same learner, same world, resampled query order/seed under the locked
acquisition rule; "meaningfully different" = paths whose transcripts
diverge at locked minimum edit distance on the reper subset —
UNRESOLVED-N5 threshold, set from L2 development data before the 2.5 lock.
The five-arm separation is preserved unchanged: I find no confound in it,
and no smaller factorial with the same identifiability exists — dropping
D loses ledger-sufficiency; dropping E loses traceability; dropping B
loses the channel split.

## 8. Level 2.5 and Level 3 roles

**Level 2.5 (C5).** Two *training* arms under matched reward budget:
(i) destination credit — reward for verified answers; (ii) path-invariant
credit — ledger entries weighted by survival across the learner's own
resampled paths (line 12's detector as training signal). Endpoint: the C3
transfer metric of the resulting structure. Role: decides mechanism
strength only (§4); required for the strong bundle-of-paths thesis, not
for `PROOF_BOUNDED`. Needs **new locked arms** (a training-signal change
is a new experiment); reuses the Level 2 world contract, harness, and
escrow machinery unchanged. Runs only on families that passed one-door
screening (§5) — path credit is meaningless where the world has one door.

**Level 3 (C6).** Computed **from existing checkpoints** of Levels 0 and
2 — no new training programme: cross-world ledger-compression event
(does one description shorten old entries across worlds), Fourier/basis
diagnostics, hidden-progress prediction. Each measure faces the two
deferred nulls before any trust: random-label (Level 0's R-0 plus Level 2
random-label diagnostic arms — cheap, added as diagnostic-only runs) and
shuffled-checkpoint-order. Constitutionally non-decisive (R5): C6 cannot
upgrade, rescue, or veto a programme verdict. What needs new locked arms:
nothing except the Level 2 random-label diagnostic runs; everything else
reads saved artifacts.
