## The programme question that must not drift

Can a small learner trained from scratch in a derivable algebraic/geometric
world acquire **primary experience**, where experience is not mere fit or
compression but structure that:

1. is earned through declared contact rather than inherited examples;
2. can be traced to a sparse ledger of prediction-breaking contact;
3. reduces the cost of reaching truth in later, unseen world families;
4. survives a semantics-preserving change of world or representation; and
5. is not reproduced by a false ledger, destination-only credit, generator
   leakage, or correlated copies of one path?

The requested result is epistemological register (b): a bounded demonstrated
principle or a precise falsification/boundary. It is not the later dream of a
foundation model raised without inherited language.

## Load-bearing ambiguity you must resolve

Level 1 proposes active equality queries versus a static corpus under equal
oracle budget and "equal answer information." Equal binary answer entropy is
not automatically equal information about the hidden structure. Matching full
mutual information may, conversely, match away the property active choice is
supposed to test.

Give a formal estimand and matching construction that:

- prevents active learning from winning merely by selecting a different label
  balance or easier query stratum;
- does not give the static arm the active policy's trajectory after the fact;
- does not use outcome-dependent matching;
- keeps query geometry/topology as the candidate causal contribution of chosen
  contact rather than an uncontrolled confound;
- can be generated and audited before outcome;
- states what a Level 1 null does and does not falsify.

If this comparison is not identifiable as currently framed, say so and replace
it with the smallest identifiable experiment. Do not paper over the gap with a
metric name.

## Required design work

### A. Build the claim DAG

At minimum distinguish these nodes; rename or split them if needed:

- **C0 Platform:** the learner/platform can exhibit delayed generalization.
  Earned by Level 0; never reuse it as programme evidence.
- **C1 Chosen contact:** choosing queries adds something beyond a matched static
  contact record.
- **C2 Forward shortening:** retained history reduces budget-to-truth on unseen
  world families relative to scratch.
- **C3 Transfer:** the reduction survives a semantics-preserving change of
  family or algebraic-to-Cayley-graph interface.
- **C4 Ledger causality:** a truthful ledger adds value not carried by weights
  alone, a ledger without the relevant weights, or a false ledger.
- **C5 Path credit:** credit for invariants surviving resampled paths adds
  transferable structure beyond matched destination-only credit.
- **C6 Balcony diagnostic:** a cross-world compression/progress measure tracks
  or predicts a transition above random-label and shuffled-order controls.

For every node specify:

- the exact operational claim;
- whether it is necessary for the essay's strong Proof;
- the minimum evidence that earns it;
- its registered kill;
- what a negative result falsifies;
- what it leaves alive;
- dependencies on earlier nodes.

Answer explicitly: does `active <= static` kill the entire manufactured-
experience thesis, or only the claim that *choice of contact* matters? If the
programme can still succeed through passive but first-hand contact, the current
roadmap overstates Level 1's logical role.
